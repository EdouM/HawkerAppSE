Cannes Film Festival Announces Lineup

LONDON — “It’s Only the End of the World,” by the upstart Canadian director Xavier Dolan and “The Last Face,” a Sean Penn movie set in Africa, are among the movies that will compete for the Palme d’Or at this year’s Cannes Film Festival, festival officials said on Thursday.

The roughly 50 films selected for various prizes and showings were announced by the festival’s director, and by the TV executive Pierre Lescure at a news conference on Thursday. Protesters demonstrating against working conditions in the French movie industry briefly delayed the presentation.

The 69th edition of the festival runs from May 11 to 22. The Australian director George Miller (of the “Mad Max” movies) will lead the jury.

Other movies that will compete for the Palme d’Or, the festival’s top prize, include “Loving,” by the American director Jeff Nichols; the South Korean director Park Chan-wook’s “The Handmaiden;” and “The Neon Demon” by the Danish director Nicolas Winding Refn.

“Dheepan,” a drama about Sri Lankan refugees directed by Jacques Audiard, won the Palme d’Or last year.

Festival organizers announced last month that Woody Allen’s “Café Society,” about 1930s Hollywood, would open the festival, as an out-of-competition film.

Steven Spielberg’s “The BFG,” starring Mark Rylance and based on a Roald Dahl children’s book, will be shown out of competition, the festival said. Other films out of the main competition lineup will include “Money Monster,” directed by Jodie Foster, and starring Julia Roberts and George Clooney and “Last Days of Louis XIV,” by the Spanish director Albert Serra.
